## The operating issue

UK commercial brokers rarely have a single document problem. They have a connected workflow-control problem.

A renewal, MTA, placement, claim, bordereaux process, or compliance file review may involve the BMS, Outlook, SharePoint, insurer portals, Excel schedules, signed PDFs, finance records, client instructions, underwriter correspondence, and compliance evidence.

The cost is measurable:

| Issue                     | Operational impact                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Split client files        | BMS, SharePoint, Outlook and portals hold different versions of the same risk record                                                           |
| Manual document collation | Account handlers spend time assembling files instead of managing client and market activity                                                    |
| Late error detection      | Missing claims histories, outdated schedules, unsigned declarations, unresolved subjectivities or inconsistent sums insured are found too late |
| Weak workflow visibility  | Operations teams cannot see document status, owner, approval stage or exception reason across the full cycle                                   |
| Compliance drag           | Reviewers spend time locating evidence before they can assess file quality                                                                     |
| Scaling constraint        | Growth increases administrative workload unless headcount rises with volume                                                                    |

AI Beaver addresses these issues through custom-built automation around the broker’s real document estate.

## Why this matters for scaling

Hiring more administrators, handlers, finance staff or reviewers is not the only way to increase capacity.

A broker handling 1,000 complex renewals per year at an average of 3 hours of administration per file spends approximately **3,000 hours** annually on renewal document handling. Reducing that to 1 hour per file releases approximately **2,000 hours**, or around **one full-time equivalent** of operational capacity.

A broker handling 5,000 MTAs per year at 35 minutes per MTA spends approximately **2,917 hours** annually on MTA handling. Reducing that to 12 minutes releases approximately **1,917 hours**, again close to **one full-time equivalent**.

The same principle applies to compliance reviews, claims evidence packs, bordereaux and finance reconciliations: capacity is released by reducing repeated document handling, not by removing control.
