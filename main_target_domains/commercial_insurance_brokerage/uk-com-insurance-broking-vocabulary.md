# UK Commercial Insurance Broking — Vocabulary Reference

A working glossary of terms in routine professional use across mid-size UK commercial insurance brokers (typically: FCA-authorised firms placing SME and mid-market business, with some access to the London/Lloyd's market either directly or via wholesale brokers).

Organised by functional area. Only established industry terms — no invented or marketing-coined acronyms.

A note on scepticism: insurance vocabulary contains a lot of overlapping terms (E&O ≈ PI, Excess ≈ Deductible, EML ≈ MFL ≈ PML), Lloyd's-specific dialects, and acronyms that mean different things in life vs. general insurance. I've flagged the common ambiguities.

## 16. Acronym Clashes Worth Knowing

Insurance loves overloaded acronyms. The common ones:

- **PI** = Professional Indemnity (in broker context); also Personal Injury (in claims/legal context).
- **EL** = Employers' Liability; in some markets, also Excess Layer.
- **CL** = Commercial Lines; also Confidential Limit or Claims Liability in other contexts.
- **GL** = General Liability (US term, equivalent to UK PL); also Group Life.
- **LOC** = Letter of Credit (often used as collateral); also Loss of Control (engineering).
- **MGA** = Managing General Agent; sometimes Managing General Agency.
- **RI** = Reinsurance; not to be confused with PI.
- **CAR** = Contractors All Risks; in non-insurance context obviously a vehicle.
- **PL** = Public Liability (UK); Product Liability (US/other markets).
- **TPA** = Third Party Administrator; also Third Party Auto in claims context.

## Terms I'd treat with scepticism

Common but loosely used, especially in sales decks:

- **InsurTech** — covers everything from genuine API platforms to lightly-rebadged brokers. Worth asking what the technology actually replaces.
- **Holistic risk solution / consultative broking** — usually marketing copy unless tied to a specific deliverable (stewardship report, BI valuation, risk register).
- **Total cost of risk (TCOR)** — legitimate concept (premium + retained losses + admin + control costs), but often quoted without the components.
- **Parametric** — real product, narrow application (mainly catastrophe, weather, cyber business interruption). Watch for it being applied where it isn't appropriate.
- **Captive feasibility** — a real exercise, but heavily oversold; for most UK mid-market clients, simpler retention structures are more cost-effective.
- **Digital placement** — means very different things on PPL vs. an SME e-trade platform vs. a quote-and-bind API.

## Caveats and Limitations of This Glossary

- **The Lloyd's/London market section is a moving target.** Blueprint Two, Velonetic (formerly DXC/LIMOSS-related processing), and the relationship between PPL and Whitespace are all in flux. The current shape is captured here, but anything operational should be checked against the LMA or LIIBA's latest briefings.
- **Regulatory entries are current as of late 2025/early 2026.** Consumer Duty rules and Solvency UK have been moving — anyone using this for compliance work should verify against the FCA Handbook and current PRA position rather than rely on a glossary.
- **Some terms blur across UK and US usage.** Excess vs. deductible, E&O vs. PI, GL vs. PL — the overlap is noted rather than one side picked, because brokers placing into Bermuda, US, or international markets will encounter both.
- **Excluded:** life, pensions, and pure personal lines vocabulary, even though brokers may touch them — they are separate worlds with their own large glossaries (GMP, SIPP, IDD-life rules, etc.).
- **Specialty classes have far deeper vocabularies than shown here** — marine (IUMI, ITCH, IVCH clauses), financial lines (FI BBB wordings, side-A DIC), construction (Phoenix clauses, ICE conditions), and W&I (TIP, breach of warranty). Each warrants a focused expansion if needed.
