# UK Mid-Market Commercial Insurance Brokers Quantity Estimate

**Question:** How many mid-market UK commercial insurance brokers exist as of May 2026?

**Best estimate as of May 2026: about 170 UK mid-market commercial insurance broker firms.**

A defensible range is **150–200 firms**, depending on how strictly “commercial insurance broker” is defined. If the whole official SIC category for insurance agents and brokers is counted, including mixed/personal-lines businesses, the upper-bound number is closer to **190–195 enterprises** with 50–249 staff.

## Evidence and implications

| Step | Evidence | Implication |
|---|---:|---|
| Official industry universe | ONS/Nomis uses SIC **66220 / 6622**, “Activities of insurance agents and brokers,” with enterprise counts by employment size band. The latest official ONS business snapshot available by May 2026 is from **14 March 2025**. | Best official base dataset, but it includes agents, brokers, commercial, personal, and mixed firms. |
| 2023 medium-size anchor | ONS’s Section K finance table for SIC 6622 shows **130 enterprises with 50–99 staff** and **65 with 100–249 staff**, giving **195 enterprises** in the 50–249 band. | Broad “insurance agents and brokers” mid-market count was about **195** in 2023. |
| 2025 adjustment | The 2025 ONS SIC 6622 total appears as **3,930 enterprises**, down from **4,035** in the 2023 detailed row, implying a similar-size-band estimate of roughly **190** if the medium-size share stayed stable. | Broad official ceiling for 50–249 staff in 2025/May 2026 is about **190**. |
| 2026 market cross-check | IBISWorld’s January 2026 industry page reports **1,770 insurance agents and brokers businesses** and **72,369 employees**, with an average of **40.9 employees per business**. | Supports a mid-market count in the low hundreds, not thousands. |
| Commercial-broker filter | BIBA says brokers arrange **94% of commercial insurance business**, but its membership and sector stats do not provide a 50–250-staff firm count. | Most mid-sized general insurance brokers are commercial-active, but not every SIC 6622 mid-sized firm should be counted as a commercial broker. |

## Conclusion

For practical market sizing, use:

**≈170 mid-market UK commercial insurance brokers**  
with a working range of **150–200**.

Interpretation:

- **~190–195** = broad ONS-style count of UK insurance agents/brokers with 50–249 staff.
- **~160–185** = likely commercial-active broker universe after excluding some personal-lines, affinity, scheme, agent, and non-core entities.
- **~150–170** = stricter target universe if “commercial insurance broker” means primarily commercial, client-advisory broker firms rather than all mixed/general insurance intermediaries.

This is an estimate, not a registry count, because there is no public May 2026 dataset that directly combines **UK**, **commercial insurance broking**, and **50–250 staff** at firm level.

## Notes on methodology

The estimate starts with the official SIC 6622 / 66220 universe, which captures “activities of insurance agents and brokers.” This category is broader than commercial insurance broking because it can include personal-lines intermediaries, affinity schemes, agents, and mixed businesses.

The broad official estimate is then filtered conceptually to a commercial-broker target universe. Because there is no public, firm-level May 2026 source that maps commercial-broker status to staff size, the final figure is expressed as a market-sizing estimate rather than a precise count.
