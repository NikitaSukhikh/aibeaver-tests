# UK Mid-Size Manufacturing — Vocabulary Reference

A working glossary of terms in routine professional use across mid-size UK manufacturers. Organised by functional area. Definitions are deliberately short — one line where possible.

A note on scepticism: a fair number of terms below (Industry 4.0, Digital Twin, Agile Manufacturing, Smart Factory) are partly genuine concepts and partly consultancy buzzwords. They appear because they're commonly used, not because every usage is meaningful. I've flagged the worst offenders.

| Term | Expansion | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| MRP | Material Requirements Planning | Logic that calculates what materials to order and when, based on BOM, inventory and demand. |
| MRP II | Manufacturing Resource Planning | Extension of MRP to include labour, machines, finance. |
| ERP | Enterprise Resource Planning | Integrated system covering finance, manufacturing, supply chain, HR. (e.g. SAP, Oracle, Microsoft Dynamics, Epicor, IFS, Sage X3.) |

| S&OP | Sales and Operations Planning | Monthly cross-functional reconciliation of demand and supply. |
| IBP | Integrated Business Planning | S&OP extended to include finance and strategy. |
| RCCP | Rough-Cut Capacity Planning | High-level capacity check on the MPS. |
| CRP | Capacity Requirements Planning | Detailed capacity check at work-centre level. |
| BOM | Bill of Materials | Hierarchical list of components needed to make a product. |
| Routing | — | The sequence of operations and work centres to make a part. |
| Work Order / Job / Production Order | — | An authorised instruction to produce a specific quantity. |
| WIP | Work In Progress (or Process) | Unfinished goods currently on the shop floor. |
| Takt Time | — | Available production time divided by customer demand rate. |
| Cycle Time | — | Time to complete one unit at a process. |
| Lead Time | — | Total elapsed time from order to delivery. |
| Throughput | — | Output rate of a process or plant. |
| Bottleneck | — | The constraint that limits overall system throughput. |
| Backflushing | — | Posting material consumption automatically when a finished good is reported. |
| Phantom BOM | — | A non-stocked subassembly used only for planning structure. |

## Terms I'd treat with scepticism

Used widely but often loosely:

- **Smart Factory, Industry 4.0, Digital Transformation** — meaningful when tied to specific outcomes (e.g. OEE uplift, reduced unplanned downtime). Empty when not.
- **Agile Manufacturing** — borrowed from software; rarely defined the same way twice.
- **Digital Twin** — legitimate as a live data-driven simulation; commonly misapplied to any 3D CAD model.
- **Lean transformation / Operational Excellence** — fine as descriptions; suspicious as consultancy products with no measurable target.
- **AI-driven [anything] in manufacturing** — most current deployments are conventional analytics rebadged. Worth asking what the model is and what decision it changes.
