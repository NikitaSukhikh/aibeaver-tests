# UK make-to-order / engineer-to-order manufacturers with 50–300 staff, May 2026

## Estimated answer

I would size the UK market at **about 3,100 make-to-order / engineer-to-order manufacturing enterprises with 50–300 staff**, with a practical planning range of **2,700–3,600**.

There is **no official UK statistic that directly labels firms as MTO/ETO, bespoke quoting, or high-RFQ-volume**. The estimate below is modelled from official UK Business Counts / ONS business-size data and SIC-sector proxies. The category rows **overlap**; they should not be added together.

| Category | Estimated UK enterprises, 50–300 staff, MTO/ETO | Practical range | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Precision engineering firms | **750** | **650–900** | CNC machining, toolmaking, high-tolerance subcontract engineering, jigs, fixtures, machined assemblies. |
| Industrial equipment manufacturers | **650** | **550–800** | Machinery, production equipment, process equipment, special-purpose equipment, repair/install-led OEMs. |
| Metal fabrication businesses | **900** | **750–1,100** | Fabricated metal products, structural/sheet metal, enclosures, welded assemblies, jobbing fabrication. |
| Packaging machinery manufacturers | **85** | **60–120** | Niche subset of machinery/equipment manufacturers focused on packaging, filling, labelling, converting, end-of-line automation. |
| Specialist component manufacturers | **550** | **450–700** | Custom components in electronics, electrical equipment, plastics/rubber, automotive, aerospace, transport and engineered subassemblies. |
| Furniture/joinery manufacturers with bespoke quoting | **275** | **200–375** | Bespoke commercial furniture, architectural joinery, fitted interiors, contract joinery manufacturers. |
| Contract manufacturers with high RFQ / order-document volume | **575** | **450–750** | Cross-sector subcontract manufacturers handling drawings, RFQs, POs, BoMs, specifications, revisions and compliance documents. |

**Deduplicated total:** approximately **3,100** firms, not the sum of the rows. A precision engineering subcontractor, for example, may also count as a metal fabrication business and a contract manufacturer.

## Basis for the estimate

The latest official UK enterprise-size source identified was **UK Business Counts 2025**, not a May 2026 census. Nomis/ONS describes the dataset as covering UK businesses, using IDBR enterprises live at the March reference date and broken down by detailed SIC code and employment-size band, including 50–99, 100–249 and 250–499 bands.

For the overall denominator, ONS reported **2.73 million VAT/PAYE-based UK businesses in March 2025** and around **130,000 registered manufacturing businesses** in the UK broad manufacturing category. An ONS ad hoc table also exists for enterprises by SIC and employee-size band as at March 2025, but it still does not identify MTO/ETO operating model, RFQ intensity or bespoke quoting.

For the 50–300-staff manufacturing base, I used ONS-derived UK Business Counts manufacturing size data. England had **111,370 manufacturing enterprises in 2025**, including **5,010 medium enterprises with 50–249 staff** and **1,010 large enterprises with 250+ staff**. The same source defines medium as 50–249 and large as 250+. Scaling England to the UK manufacturing base and adding only the estimated 250–300 slice of the 250+ band gives an approximate UK base of **about 6,000 manufacturing enterprises with 50–300 staff**.

I then applied MTO/ETO propensity by sector. The strongest relevant SIC proxies are fabricated metals, machinery/equipment, repair and installation of machinery, computer/electronic/optical products, electrical equipment, furniture, wood products and “other manufacturing”. In the ONS-derived manufacturing breakdown for England, “advanced engineering” is **45.5%** of manufacturing enterprises; fabricated metal products alone are **16.5%**, machinery/equipment **5.4%**, repair/installation of machinery **11.2%**, furniture **5.2%**, and wood products **7.5%**.

The resulting estimate is that roughly **half of the 50–300-staff manufacturing base** is meaningfully make-to-order, engineer-to-order, bespoke-quote or RFQ/document-heavy. That gives the **2,700–3,600** deduplicated range, with **~3,100** as the central estimate.

## Caveats

- The figures are **estimates**, not official counts.
- MTO/ETO status, bespoke quoting and RFQ/order-document intensity are not directly visible in ONS business-count tables.
- The category estimates overlap and should not be summed.
- The practical range reflects uncertainty in sector classification, company operating model and the 250–300 staff slice of the 250+ size band.
